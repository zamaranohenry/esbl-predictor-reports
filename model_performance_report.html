2. Metric Definitions

The following metrics are reported for each model across training, validation, and test splits:

MetricFormula / DefinitionInterpretation in ESBL context
AUROC (Area Under ROC Curve) Probability that the model ranks a random ESBL+ patient higher than a random ESBL- patient Primary metric. 1.0 = perfect; 0.5 = random. Values ≥0.80 indicate strong discrimination.
Accuracy (TP + TN) / (TP + TN + FP + FN) Overall correct classifications. Can be misleading with class imbalance — use alongside Precision/Recall.
Precision TP / (TP + FP) Of patients flagged as ESBL+, what fraction truly are. High precision → fewer unnecessary broad-spectrum treatments.
Recall (Sensitivity) TP / (TP + FN) Of patients who truly have ESBL, what fraction are correctly identified. High recall → fewer missed ESBL cases. Critical for patient safety.
F1-Score 2 × (Precision × Recall) / (Precision + Recall) Harmonic mean of Precision and Recall. Balances both concerns. Secondary selection metric.
MCC (Matthews Correlation Coefficient) Ranges −1 to +1; accounts for all four confusion matrix cells Most informative single metric for binary classification on imbalanced datasets. Values >0.5 indicate good classification.

8. Clinical Interpretation

Best model: Logistic Regression
Test AUROC = 0.9062  |  Test Accuracy = 82.0%  |  Test F1 = 82.0%

What the AUROC means clinically

An AUROC of 0.9062 means that if a clinician randomly selected one ESBL-positive and one ESBL-negative patient from this test cohort, the model would rank the ESBL-positive patient higher approximately 90.6% of the time.

Threshold and operating point

The model outputs a probability score (0–100%). The default classification threshold is 50% — patients with a predicted probability ≥50% are classified as High Risk. This threshold can be adjusted by clinical teams depending on whether higher sensitivity (fewer missed ESBL cases) or higher specificity (fewer false alarms) is preferred.

Limitations

  • The model was trained on a single-institution dataset (MRRH). Performance may differ at other sites.
  • Only 660 of 1,569 patients had confirmed culture results and were used for training. The unlabelled 909 were excluded from modelling.
  • Post-culture features (antibiogram, isolate species, specimen type) were excluded to ensure the model is applicable at the point of empirical treatment decision.
  • This tool is a decision-support aid and does not replace clinical judgement. All predictions must be interpreted by qualified healthcare professionals.
